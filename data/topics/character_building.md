# Character classes, ascendancies, skill gems, and socket links

## Character Classes & Starting Positions

Path of Exile features 7 base classes, each positioned at different points on the shared passive skill tree (~1300 nodes total). Your class choice determines your starting location and available ascendancies, but doesn't lock you into specific skills or playstyles.

### The Seven Classes

| Class | Attribute Focus | Starting Area | Typical Builds |
|-------|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Witch | Intelligence | Top-left (caster nodes) | Elementalist spells, Necromancer minions/auras |
| Templar | Str/Int hybrid | Top-center | Elemental/melee hybrids, totems, auras |
| Marauder | Strength | Top-right (melee/life nodes) | Pure melee, slams, totems |
| Duelist | Str/Dex hybrid | Right-center | Melee, bow builds, bleed/impale |
| Ranger | Dexterity | Bottom-right (bow/evasion nodes) | Bow builds, melee dex scaling |
| Shadow | Dex/Int hybrid | Bottom-center | Crit, poison, traps/mines |
| Scion | All attributes | Center | Hybrid builds using multiple areas |

> **Key Insight:** While classes suggest certain builds, the passive tree is shared — a Witch can go melee if they path to those nodes, though it's inefficient.

## Ascendancies: Your Build Identity

Each class (except Scion) has 3 ascendancies unlocked through the Labyrinth. This is your most crucial build decision — ascendancies provide powerful, unique mechanics unavailable elsewhere.

**Witch:**
- Elementalist: Elemental damage, golems, ailment immunity
- Necromancer: Minions, auras, corpse mechanics
- Occultist: Chaos damage, energy shield, curses, cold DoT

**Templar:**
- Guardian: Support/aura builds, defensive minions
- Hierophant: Totems, mana/MoM, brands
- Inquisitor: Elemental damage, ignores resistances, consecrated ground

**Marauder:**
- Juggernaut: Maximum defense, endurance charges, unstoppable
- Berserker: Maximum offense, rage mechanic, leech
- Chieftain: Fire damage, totems, life recovery, covered in ash

**Duelist:**
- Gladiator: Block, bleed, explosions, dual wield
- Champion: Impale, fortify, accuracy, intimidate
- Slayer: Two-handed weapons, overleech, area damage, culling

**Ranger:**
- Raider: Speed, frenzy charges, elemental damage, onslaught
- Deadeye: Projectiles, chains, far shot, mirage archer
- Pathfinder: Flasks, chaos damage, poison, elemental damage

**Shadow:**
- Assassin: Critical strikes, poison, power charges
- Trickster: DoT, energy shield, evasion, recovery
- Saboteur: Traps, mines, blind, area damage

**Scion:**
- Ascendant: Access to mini-versions of other ascendancies plus passive tree start points
- Reliquarian: Unique item-inspired passive nodes with unprecedented build flexibility, uniques rotate every league

> **Pro Tip:** Ascendancy choice often matters more than starting class. A Necromancer summoner and an Elementalist caster are completely different builds despite both being Witches.

## Skill Gems: Your Abilities

Skills in PoE come from gems socketed into gear. There are two types:

### Active Skill Gems

These create your usable abilities. Examples:
- **Fireball:** Projectile spell that deals fire damage
- **Cyclone:** Melee attack that spins while channeling
- **Summon Skeletons:** Minion spell that raises skeleton warriors
- **Lightning Strike:** Melee attack that fires lightning projectiles

Each active gem has:
- Damage scaling (spell damage, attack damage, minion damage, etc.)
- Required attributes (Str/Dex/Int requirements)
- Level scaling (damage, mana cost, requirements increase per level)

### Support Gems

These modify active skills and only work when linked. Examples:
- **Melee Physical Damage Support:** 49% more melee physical damage at gem level 20
- **Multistrike Support:** Melee attacks repeat 2 additional times
- **Spell Echo Support:** Spell skills repeat an additional time
- **Minion Damage Support:** 49% more minion damage

> **Critical Rule:** Support gems only affect active gems they're linked to in sockets.

## Socket Links: The Foundation of Power

Your gear has sockets (white/red/green/blue) that hold gems. Sockets can be linked together — supports only affect actives they share links with.

### Link Configurations

- **1-Link:** Just the active skill (minimal damage)
- **4-Link:** Active + 3 supports (decent damage, league start viable)
- **5-Link:** Active + 4 supports (early endgame)
- **6-Link:** Active + 5 supports (optimal endgame setup)

### Socket Colors & Requirements

- **Red** sockets: Strength gems (melee attacks, defensive supports)
- **Green** sockets: Dexterity gems (bow skills, movement, utility)
- **Blue** sockets: Intelligence gems (spells, auras, elemental supports)
- **White** sockets: Any color gem (rare, valuable)

Chromatic Orbs reroll socket colors. Higher attribute requirements make certain colors more likely.

### Link Examples

4-Link Fireball Setup:
```
[Fireball] - [Spell Echo] - [Added Lightning Damage] - [Faster Casting]
```

6-Link Melee Setup:
```
[Cyclone] - [Melee Phys Damage] - [Fortify] - [Impale] - [Multistrike] - [Maim]
```

Aura Setup (doesn't need links):
```
[Hatred] [Herald of Ash] [Precision] [Blood and Sand]
```

## Advanced Linking Concepts

### More vs Increased

- **More** multipliers stack multiplicatively (support gems typically give "more")
- **Increased** modifiers stack additively (passive tree typically gives "increased")
- More multipliers are exponentially more powerful

### Support Gem Priority

Not all supports are equal. Typical priority for damage:

1. More damage supports (Melee Phys, Elemental Focus, etc.)
2. Utility with damage (Multistrike, Spell Echo)
3. Quality of life (Faster Attacks/Casting)
4. Pure utility (Fortify, Blind)

### Meta-Gaming Links

- **Trigger setups:** Cast on Crit, Cast when Damage Taken
- **Aura stacking:** Multiple auras for support builds
- **Weapon swapping:** Different 6-links for different situations

## Practical Build Planning

Questions to ask:

1. What's my main damage skill? This gets the 6-link priority.
2. What ascendancy synergizes? Match your skill to ascendancy bonuses.
3. What supports scale my damage type? Physical vs elemental vs chaos have different supports.
4. What defensive layers do I need? Save sockets for defensive skills/auras.
5. How many active skills can I realistically use? Socket pressure is real — prioritize.

Socket planning priority:

1. **Main damage skill:** 6-link with optimal supports
2. **Movement skill:** 3-4 link (Dash + Faster Attacks + Fortify)
3. **Auras:** Often don't need links, but reservation efficiency helps
4. **Defensive skills:** Guard skills, curses, defensive auras
5. **Utility:** Golem, banner, exposure skills

> **Endgame Reality:** You'll have ~30 sockets across all gear. Plan accordingly — every socket has opportunity cost.

## Key Takeaways

- Ascendancy choice defines your build identity more than starting class
- 6-link main skill is non-negotiable for endgame damage scaling
- Support gem selection can make or break a build — research optimal setups
- Socket colors matter — plan your stat requirements around available sockets
- More multipliers scale exponentially — each additional support gem is ~20-30% more damage
- Socket pressure is real — you can't fit everything, so prioritize ruthlessly
